If you do not agree with this Policy, please do not use our services or provide us with your personal data.

1. Information We Collect

We collect personal data necessary to provide our digital and physical services. This information may include:

  • Personal Identifiers: Name, email address, phone number, date of birth, gender, and national identification details (e.g., NIN, BVN, passport number) required for identity verification, financial integrations, or consultancy.
  • Corporate & Business Data: For Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) services, we collect proposed business names, business objectives, shareholder/director details, home and corporate addresses, and valid government-issued identification media.
  • Professional & Career Data: For freelancing setup and digital branding services, we collect professional resumes, portfolio links, employment histories, specific digital skill sets, and temporary platform credentials.
  • Contact and Communication Data: Physical addresses, WhatsApp contact details, and any transcripts or messages you send us via digital forms or email.
  • Service-Specific Academic Data: Educational records, examination scores, registration details, and admission letters for portal services (e.g., JAMB, Post-UTME, WAEC/NECO GCE, NOUN).
  • Technical & Network Configuration Data: IP address, browser type, device information, usage patterns, and server/network topology preferences for cloud storage setup or remote collaboration tools.
  • Sensitive Personal Data: Where absolutely necessary (e.g., biometric data for specific identity verifications), we collect and process this data only with your explicit, separate consent and in strict line with NDPA requirements.

We collect data directly from you (via online forms, emails, chats, or in-person interactions), from third parties (such as government portals like NIMC, JAMB, or CAC during processing), or automatically through essential cookies and usage tracking technologies.

2. How We Use Your Information

We process your personal data on lawful bases under the NDPA, primarily your explicit consent, contractual necessity to deliver a service, or our legitimate business interests. Uses include:

  • Fulfilling Services: Processing your corporate registration with the CAC, registering you for academic examinations (JAMB/Post-UTME), printing authorized identity slips, configuring cloud storage instances, or building custom web structures.
  • Account Optimization & Onboarding: Setting up and optimizing professional freelancer profiles on global remote work platforms.
  • Communications: Reaching out via email, phone, or WhatsApp to share project updates, service confirmations, technical handovers, or support feedback.
  • Educational Delivery: Facilitating live, private 1-on-1 coaching and skills training sessions.
  • Compliance & Legal Obligations: Meeting necessary reporting protocols mandated by regulatory bodies like the NDPC, NITDA, or financial compliance laws.
  • Marketing Integration: Sending tailored newsletters or promotional updates regarding new digital offerings, provided you have opted-in to receive them.

We adhere strictly to data minimization principles, gathering only the exact data volume required to execute your request.

4. Data Storage, Security, and Transfers

  • Storage Localization: Your data is securely archived in encrypted digital systems in Nigeria, or with globally trusted, NDPA-compliant cloud storage providers that maintain stringent data localization safeguards.
  • Technical Security Measures: We implement advanced technical and organizational guardrails—including Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) encryption, firewalls, closed access controls, and regular data audits—to minimize breach risks.
  • Cross-Border Data Transfers: When setting up global freelancing profiles or international payment channels, your data may pass across international borders. We ensure all such transfers fully comply with the cross-border transfer mechanisms and safeguards mandated by the NDPA 2023.
  • Breach Notifications: In the highly unlikely event of a structural data breach, we are legally bound to notify you and the NDPC within the timelines required by law.

5. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third-party marketers. We share your information exclusively with:

  • Essential Service Authorities: Government entities (such as NIMC, JAMB, WAEC, and the CAC) and global professional or payment networks required to complete your ordered services.
  • Trusted Subcontractors: Core IT vendors, web hosting systems, or secure digital invoice platforms operating under strict legal confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal & Law Enforcement: Competent Nigerian courts or regulatory compliance authorities where legally required by an official court order or statutory mandate.

6. Your Data Rights

Under the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023, you retain powerful rights over your information, which include:

  • Right to Access: Request an explicit copy of all personal records we hold regarding you.
  • Right to Rectification: Request immediate correction of inaccurate, outdated, or incomplete profiles.
  • Right to Erasure ("To be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your records, subject to conflicting legal or tax retention obligations.
  • Right to Object & Restrict: Limit or object to how we process your information based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to Data Portability: Obtain your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format to move elsewhere.

To exercise any of these protections, please contact our team (see Section 9). We respond to legitimate requests within one calendar month.
